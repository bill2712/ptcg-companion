## What is Crema?
Crema is the dense, golden-brown foam layer on top of espresso. It is the product of emulsification (Emulsification) of the high-pressure carbon dioxide inside the coffee beans with the oil and water of the coffee itself under the high-pressure extraction of 9 Bar.

## Is Crema an indicator of good coffee?
Traditionally, rich crema is regarded as a symbol of "fresh" and "well-extracted" espresso. However, modern specialty coffee science believes that although Crema provides excellent visual and taste (smoothness), it is actually very bitter (full of carbon dioxide and extremely fine powder).

## Factors affecting Crema
- **Freshness**: The fresher (unfinished degassing) the beans, the more carbon dioxide and the thicker the Crema.
- **Bean Species**: Robusta beans generally produce a thicker, longer-lasting crema than Arabica beans.
- **Roast Degree**: The oil of dark roasted beans is easier to release. Usually the crema is richer, but it also dissipates quickly.
