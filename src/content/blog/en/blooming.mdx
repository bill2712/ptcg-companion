## What is Blooming?
Steaming refers to the process of injecting a small amount of hot water (approximately 2-3 times the weight of the powder) to evenly wet the coffee powder in the early stages of hand brewing or filter drip coffee, and letting it sit for a period of time (usually 30-45 seconds). During this time, you will see the bed of coffee grounds expand with bubbles popping up.

## Why is steaming so important?
During the roasting process, coffee beans produce a large amount of carbon dioxide, which is locked inside the beans. When hot water comes into contact with coffee grounds, these gases are quickly released. If you directly add a large amount of water without steaming first, the carbon dioxide bubbles will hinder the contact between the hot water and the coffee powder, causing a "channeling effect" and uneven extraction.

## Practical Implications and Tips
- **Freshness Index**: The fresher the beans, the higher they will expand when steamed. If there is no expansion at all, the beans may have been deflated (stale).
- **Water Amount Control**: The amount of water for steaming should not be too much. Too much water will start extraction early and drip into the lower pot; too little water will not evenly moisten all the powder layers.
