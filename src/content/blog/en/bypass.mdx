## What is Bypass?
Side flow means that the hot water finds a "shortcut" in the filter cup. Instead of passing through the coffee powder bed, it flows directly into the coffee pot along the gap between the filter paper and the filter cup wall.

## The good and bad of side flow
- **THE BAD**: Unintended side flow can severely dilute your coffee (lower TDS) and result in a lower-than-expected extraction rate because less water passes through the bed. This is why teachings often say, "Don't pour water to the edge of the filter paper."
- **Benefits**: Some special brewing methods or filter cup designs (such as Tricolate, or the methods of some competition players) will deliberately use side flow or "add water Bypass" after brewing to dilute the overly strong coffee liquid to achieve a refreshing taste without bringing out the bitter taste in the later stage.

## How to control side flow?
In general hand brewing, try to control the water injection range from the center to the middle periphery of the coffee powder bed to prevent the water column from directly washing the outermost edge of the filter cup.
