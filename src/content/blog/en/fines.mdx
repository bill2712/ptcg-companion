## What are fines?
During the process of chopping coffee beans in any grinder, extremely fine powder (usually less than 100 microns in diameter) is produced. These tiny particles are called "fines."

## Double-sided blade for fine powder
- **Disadvantages (over-extraction and clogging)**: Because the surface of fine powders is so large, they will be "completely extracted" the moment they come into contact with hot water. Excessive fines are the source of bitterness in hand-brewed coffee. In addition, fine powder can easily deposit to the bottom of the filter paper driven by the water flow, blocking the capillary pores, resulting in slower flow rate and longer extraction time.
- **Advantages (Provides body)**: Coffee without fines at all tends to taste like water and lacks taste. The right proportion of fine powder can provide coffee with complexity and body. In espresso, fine powder is an essential element to build resistance and produce crema.

## Reduce the interference of fine powder
If you feel that hand-brewed coffee tends to be bitter and the flow rate is very slow, you may consider upgrading to a higher-end blade grinder or using a powder sifter to screen out some of the very fine powder.
