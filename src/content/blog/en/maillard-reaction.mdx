## What is Maillard Reaction?
The Menard reaction is one of the most fascinating chemical reactions in food science. When foods containing amino acids (proteins) and reducing sugars are heated to about 140°C to 165°C, the two react, creating hundreds of entirely new flavor compounds and turning the food an attractive brown color. The aroma of toasted bread, the charred aroma of seared steak, and the roasting of coffee beans are all attributed to the Menard reaction.

## The effect of Menard reaction on coffee
During the coffee roasting process, after the green beans pass through the dehydration stage, they will enter the Menard reaction stage. This stage determines the coffee’s “body”, “caramel sweetness” and “roasted aromas such as nuts and chocolate”.

## Considerations when brewing
The darker the roast (the more complete the Menard reaction, even entering the caramelization and carbonization stages), the more fragile the cell walls of the coffee beans are, and the easier it is for substances to be extracted. This is why when brewing **dark roasted coffee**, we usually recommend lowering the water temperature (85°C-88°C) to avoid excessive extraction and bringing out the burnt bitter taste; while **light roasted coffee** (shorter Menard reaction) requires higher temperatures (92°C-94°C) to force out the flavor.
