## What is channeling?
The channel effect means that when hot water passes through a bed of coffee powder, it does not evenly wet all the powder, but instead finds the path of least resistance (such as cracks or lower-density areas in the powder bed), forming one or more "water channels."

## Why is the channel effect a brewing killer?
When the channel effect occurs, most of the hot water will flow away from the channel. This causes the coffee powder around the channel to be severely "over-extracted", producing an extremely bitter, dry woody taste; while the coffee powder outside the channel is "under-extracted", retaining a sharp sour taste. You end up with a cup of coffee that is sour, bitter, and has a confusing flavor.

## How to avoid channel effect?
- **Even Distribution**: Make sure the coffee powder bed is flat before filling with water.
- **Improve grinding quality**: Use a high-quality Burr Grinder to reduce the gap between fines and coarse particles.
- **Stable Water Injection (Agitation)**: Avoid using too strong water column to directly impact the powder bed, causing perforation of the powder bed.
