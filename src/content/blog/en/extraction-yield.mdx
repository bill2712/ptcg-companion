## What is Extraction Yield?
Extraction rate refers to "what percentage of coffee powder substances are dissolved into the coffee liquid." About 30 percent of the material in coffee beans is soluble in water, but not all of that soluble material is delicious.

## The golden range of extraction rate
According to SCAA (Specialty Coffee Association) research, the ideal extraction rate falls between **18% and 22%**:
- **Less than 18% (under-extraction)**: Only the most easily soluble fruit acids and salts are extracted. The coffee will taste sharp sour and salty, and lack sweetness and aftertaste.
- **18% - 22% (Gold Extract)**: Balanced between sweet and sour, mellow taste, obvious flavor of origin.
- **Above 22% (over-extraction)**: Insoluble burnt bitter substances and wood fiber flavor are extracted, making the coffee taste bitter and dry.

## How to adjust the extraction rate?
You can fine-tune your extraction rate by changing **Grin Size** (the finer, the higher the extraction rate), **Water Temperature** (the higher, the higher the extraction rate), **Extraction Time** (the longer, the higher the extraction rate), and **The Disturbance Level**.
