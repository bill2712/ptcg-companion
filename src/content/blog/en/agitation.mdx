## What is Agitation?
Disturbance refers to the tumbling or moving action of the powder bed when brewing coffee. This can be achieved by the impact of the water column when filling the water, or by using a spoon or stir stick to stir manually.

## Physical meaning of disturbance
On a microscopic level, when hot water surrounds coffee grounds, the water surrounding the grounds quickly becomes saturated with coffee matter. Without disturbance, the extraction speed will be slower. Disturbance can wash away the saturated water layer on the surface of the coffee powder and replace it with fresh hot water, thus "accelerating extraction."

## How to use perturbation?
- **AeroPress/French Press**: It is usually strongly recommended to agitation (Agitation) after filling with water to ensure that all powders are fully exposed to water.
- **Hand Flush (V60)**: requires careful control. Moderate disturbance can improve the extraction rate; but excessive disturbance (such as too strong water column) will cause the fine powder to sink and block the pores of the filter paper, resulting in stagnant flow rate and bitter taste.
