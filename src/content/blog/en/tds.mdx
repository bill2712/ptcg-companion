## What is Total Dissolved Solids (TDS)?
TDS (Total Dissolved Solids) stands for "Strength" in coffee. It refers to the percentage of solid matter in a cup of brewed coffee liquid.
For example, a cup of hand-brewed coffee with a TDS of 1.3% means that the liquid in the cup is 98.7% water and 1.3% coffee flavoring substances.

## The difference between TDS and extraction rate
- **Extraction Yield** refers to how much "coffee beans" are consumed.
- **TDS** is to see how thick "this glass of water" is.
You can over-extract (bringing out the bitterness) but also add a lot of water resulting in a low TDS (tasting like bitter water).

## Ideal TDS range
- **Hand brew/drip coffee**: 1.15% - 1.45%
- **Espresso**: 8% - 12%

To change TDS, the most direct way is to adjust the **powder to water ratio**.
